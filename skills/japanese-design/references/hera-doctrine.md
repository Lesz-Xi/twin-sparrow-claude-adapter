# Hara Doctrine — Ex-Formation Centered

**Sources:** Kenya Hara — *Designing Design* (2007), *White*, MUJI work, RE-DESIGN / HAPTIC exhibitions.  
**Role in Twin-Sparrow:** Operating method for surface work. Not decorative Japan.

> Hara decides what we remove; Relics shows what remains when Twin-Sparrow is honest on the web.

---

## Core thesis

Design is not the addition of form, information, or decoration.  
Design is the **deliberate removal** of the non-essential so meaning, use, and perception can arise in the space that remains.

**Emptiness (ku)** vs Western simplicity:

| | Simplicity | Emptiness |
|--|------------|-----------|
| Action | Reduces | Removes |
| Effect | Tells the user what the thing is | Creates a receptive vessel the user fills |
| Goal | Clarity through reduction | Freedom through emptiness |

---

## Ex-formation

**Ex-formation** = the active process of *un-knowing*. Opposite of information accumulation.

1. Identify what the audience already “knows” or assumes.
2. Deliberately remove or reframe those assumptions.
3. Leave receptive space (ma) for new perception.
4. Result is not simpler — it is **stranger and more alive**.

Ex-formation is not minimalism. It is defamiliarization as a creative act.

## Lexicon (operational)

| Principle | Japanese | In design work |
|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Emptiness | Ku (空) | Receptive space, not blank leftovers |
| Interval | Ma (間) | Active gap; hairlines over boxes; vertical rhythm |
| Understatement | Shibui (渋い) | One accent; beauty arrives slowly |
| Simplicity of means | Kanso (簡素) | Few type voices; motion only when it explains hierarchy |
| Asymmetry | Fukinsei (不均整) | 7/5 and living imbalance |
| Stillness | Seijaku (静寂) | Quiet attention; no spectacle motion |

## Method (every polish pass)

### 1. Inventory assumptions
What does the viewer expect this to be? What language/decoration is “standard”?

### 2. Remove
Delete explanatory clauses that do not change understanding. Collapse labels. Strip visual noise.

### 3. Create receptive space
Increase ma. Hairlines or tonal shifts instead of boxes. Let title, proof, and claim breathe.

### 4. Test for aliveness
Does it feel newly visible? Can the viewer project thought into the space? Does it reward slow attention?

If weak → return to step 2.

---

## Checklist

- [ ] Removed at least one layer of assumed explanation?
- [ ] Generous ma around the most important elements?
- [ ] Every remaining element earns its presence?
- [ ] Motion (if any) explains hierarchy rather than performs?
- [ ] Page still functions greyscale / motion-off?
- [ ] Feels quietly alive rather than complete?
