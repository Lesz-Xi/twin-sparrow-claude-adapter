## Layout mechanisms

- **7/5 asymmetry** for heads and proof plates (not equal card grids).
- **Hairlines** separate planes; rectangular media; radius only where material logic demands (fill-up pill may be pill-shaped; proof plates are rectangular).
- **Proof plates** (projects): media/stat before meta; desc under hairline; caps as vertical ledger; status as hairline marker, not pill-dot badge.
- **Gutters from grid gap**, not meta padding chrome.
- Kinetic deck: depth from **stack offset + hairline**, not drop-shadow under the front card.

## Truthful specimen

Relics uses its own material as proof. When real Twin-Sparrow evidence exists, use it instead of generic architectural, portfolio, or lifestyle placeholders.

- Names, versions, counts, capabilities, dates, and states must be real or absent.
- A teaching specimen may abstract form, but it should remain native to the product it teaches.
- Self-reference is useful when it exposes mechanism; it becomes vanity when it only repeats branding.
- Proof is not atmosphere. If the artifact cannot carry a claim, do not frame it as evidence.

## Inspectable operation — “The trace of a turn”

One real operation is more trustworthy than a dashboard of simulated cognition.

| Trace | What it exposes |
|-------|------------------|
| Context | What entered the operation |
| Orientation | What judgment or constraint governed it |
| Action | What materially changed |
| Verification | What became demonstrably true |

Surface rule: use a quiet ledger, one active hairline, and restrained reading-band emphasis. Never turn the trace into fake chain-of-thought, animated telemetry, or a status-card grid.

## Living marginal note

A single dated field note may keep a resolved page open to becoming.

- It is authored, present-tense, and tied to a real change in the work.
- It is maintained when the product meaningfully changes or removed when stale.
- It is never an automated activity feed, synthetic diary, roadmap theater, or testimonial carousel.
- Its visual role is marginal: narrow prose, ma, one rule, and no competing action.

---

## Interaction (seijaku)

- Nav links: opacity + hairline underline reveal.
- Logo: shared size nav/footer; hover lift `translateY(-3px)` + opacity.
- Primary button: sage fill rises from below (`translateY(101%→0)`); no glow.
- Ghost: underline reveal, no competing border box.
- Hover never invents a second primary action.

## Motion character (summary)

Full numbers and wiring: `motion-relics.md`.

| Mechanism | Intent |
|-----------|--------|
| Distance-from-center **scrub** | Clarifies near center; recedes when leaving |
| Soft blur | **Recede only** — never blur-in entrance |
| Line mask (`--ln-y`) | Display lines rise into reading plane |
| Clip-path wipe | Media/proof unmask |
| Lenis | Opt-in for long editorial; native scroll is honest baseline |
| `data-rv-stay` | Hold clear after a real center crossing (e.g. final content block that can pass above center) |
| Reduced motion | Full content, no scrub, no smooth scroll |

Motion explains hierarchy. If it performs intelligence, delete it.
